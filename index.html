Why now

Your AI features are already creating governance questions.

Once AI traffic reaches an external provider or vendor workflow, it is too late to ask basic control questions. Talon gives engineering and governance teams an enforcement point before the request leaves your environment.

🔐

What customer data left?

Detect, classify, redact, warn, or block PII before the model call. Keep evidence of what was detected without storing raw prompts by default.

🛠️

What tools could the agent use?

Filter, allow, or block risky tools and MCP calls so destructive or bulk operations are controlled before execution.

💶

Who is spending money?

Apply per-caller budgets and model allowlists before forwarding the request, not only after spend is logged.

Who it is for

Built for European SMB teams putting AI into production.

Talon is strongest when a team already has an AI feature, support bot, internal agent, or vendor AI workflow and needs practical runtime controls without rebuilding the product.

Fast integration

Start with one workflow. Keep your existing app.

Your application can keep using the same OpenAI-compatible SDK. Point the base URL at Talon, send a Talon caller key, and let Talon inject the approved provider credential from its vault.

Add Talon to an existing appChoose integration path
# Before
OPENAI_BASE_URL=https://api.openai.com/v1
OPENAI_API_KEY=sk-provider-key

# After
OPENAI_BASE_URL=http://talon:8080/v1/proxy/openai/v1
OPENAI_API_KEY=<talon-caller-key>

# Then inspect proof
talon audit list
talon audit verify <evidence-id>
